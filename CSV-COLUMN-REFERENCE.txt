METRIC DEFINITIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Sharpe Ratio:
  Measures risk-adjusted return
  Formula: Average Return / Volatility
  Higher = Better risk-adjusted performance
  Typical range: -1.0 to 3.0
  Good: > 1.0

Maximum Drawdown:
  Largest peak-to-trough decline
  Formula: (Peak - Trough) / Peak × 100
  Lower = Better downside protection
  Expressed as percentage
  Good: < 10%

Volatility:
  Standard deviation of returns
  Measures consistency/stability
  Lower = More stable performance
  Expressed as percentage
  Good: < 20%

Win Rate:
  Percentage of profitable trades
  Formula: Winning Trades / Total Trades × 100
  Higher = More consistent wins
  Typical range: 30% - 70%
  Good: > 50%

Number of Trades:
  Total buy + sell actions
  Indicates trading frequency
  More active traders have higher counts

Profit Factor:
  Ratio of gross profits to gross losses
  Formula: Total Gains / Total Losses
  Above 1.0 = Net profitable
  Typical range: 0.5 - 2.5
  Good: > 1.2

Time in Market:
  Average days holding positions
  Indicates trading style
  Higher = Buy-and-hold strategy
  Lower = Active trading strategy
  Typical range: 2-8 days

EXAMPLE ANALYSIS

- Bullish Sharpe: 0.95  (Strong in uptrends)
- Bearish Sharpe: -0.45 (Struggles in downtrends)
- Sideways Sharpe: 0.15 (Weak in range-bound markets)

INTERPRETATION:
→ Excels in bull markets
→ Needs better bear market strategy
→ Struggles with consolidation periods
→ Likely uses momentum-based approach

Participant B:
- Bullish Sharpe: 0.52
- Bearish Sharpe: 0.48
- Sideways Sharpe: 0.45

INTERPRETATION:
→ Consistent across all market conditions
→ Adaptable trading style
→ Lower overall returns but more stable
→ Better risk management
